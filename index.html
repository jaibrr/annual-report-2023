MSD DIRECTOR INTRO ANNUAL REPORT 2022

As we reflect upon the past year, it becomes evident that 2022 was a pivotal moment for the global Management Services (previously called “Administration”) function, propelling us forward on our journey towards achieving our ambitious goals and making a lasting impact on the lives of the most vulnerable populations across the globe, through improved delivery of support services to the whole organization.

We witnessed remarkable progress in the implementation of our five-year strategy which has served as our guiding light, illuminating the path towards enhancing our organizational effectiveness, efficiency, and resilience. With the implementation of the MSD strategy, we have improved our operational and service excellence approach and honed in our focus on people management, streamlining our processes and adopting innovative approaches to harness the power of technology to improve our efficiency.

With improved structures and awareness of who we are and what we do, we have fostered a culture of continuous improvement and collaboration, which cultivates a value-centric environment where diverse perspectives are treasured, and creativity thrives. Together, we have harnessed the power of teamwork, enabling us to overcome obstacles and achieve breakthroughs in the provision of support services to the organization under the Management Services function.

Words are not enough to describe my gratitude to be part of this community. We, the global Management Services community, are at the very core of our organization’s ability to fulfill its mandate. Our work is the foundation upon which the World Food Programme stands tall, providing support to millions of people affected by hunger and food insecurity worldwide.

I am immensely proud to lead this vital function because the passion and dedication I witness from each and every Management Services personnel provide me great inspiration to do more and give our best to the organization. This steadfast commitment to our shared vision fuels my own determination to lead us towards even greater achievements.

Finally, as we navigate the complexities of our humanitarian landscape, it is crucial that we remain united in purpose. Together, we can continue to innovate, adapt, and excel in our quest to fight hunger and build a more equitable world. The challenges ahead may be formidable, but I have every confidence that, with our function’s collective efforts and contributions to WFP, we will overcome them and create a brighter future for those who need us most.

So please join me in reading through the chapters of the 2022 Annual Report and let’s celebrate our accomplishments, acknowledge our learnings, and set our sights on the horizon of endless possibilities.

Finally, to the global Management Services team, thank you for your unwavering commitment to our mission, your unwavering dedication to the cause and your unwavering spirit in the face of adversity. Together, let us continue to work together to enable us to make a tangible difference in the lives of the most vulnerable.

Engineering

BUILDING FOR A SUSTAINABLE FUTURE

Infrastructure is key in WFP’s response to emergencies, but also in longer-term response and recovery programs. And it’s exactly here where the WFP Engineering team comes to action. School kitchens, refugee camp distribution centers, warehouses, bridges, roads, airstrips, silos, dams, and even WFP offices and accommodation, WFP Engineers support the field every day with a wide variety of services.

With presence in more than 50 countries, WFP Engineering is composed of a global community of approximately 200 engineers and technicians in the field, at RBs and in HQ who support the global operations every day to connect communities to markets, provide easier access to food and lower the risk of devastating food insecurity.

WFP Engineering work is not limited just to our organization, they also collaborate with other UN agencies who benefit from their expertise: FAO, UNHCR, UNICEF, WHO, UNDP and IOM.

Field Support and Staffing

Services that support the field and the workforce

The Field Support and Staffing branch (MSDS) went live in 2021 and started expanding in mid-2022. The focus has been on strengthening management services operations and their related performance. MSDS provides coordination on interrelated workstreams and focuses on addressing functional challenges, supporting field colleagues during emergencies both onsite and through increased preparedness, whilst providing guidance, oversight, and technical support to ongoing operations. MSDS is responsible for collating, reviewing, and updating relevant resources, including service manuals, technical tools, guidance and policy development, oversight/internal controls. Deliverables include: (i) Support oversights and compliance through the development of effective tools and guidance for functional oversights; (ii) support CO/RB MS function to augment its oversight and support capacities through provision of workshops, trainings, and exchange of best practices; (iii) ensure emergency preparedness by coordinating a rapid deployment capacity and empowering capabilities; (iv) ensure adequate staffing for the function based on a comprehensive review process.

UN FLEET

Safe, efficient, and sustainable vehicles for the UN System

UN FLEET is a joint operation between WFP and UNHCR and a tangible UN Reform initiative providing support across the United Nations system for the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development applying the three UN Reform principles of Mutual Recognition, Client Satisfaction and Costing and Pricing of Services to its business model. In 2022, UN FLEET officially launched and began operations, marking a new phase in safe, efficient, and sustainable fleet management for the UN System. The year was marked by significant milestones, as 6 Client Agencies (UNON, UNFPA, UNICEF, WHO, ILO and OCHA) signed Global Service Agreements with UN FLEET and the first 27 leased vehicles were delivered to Client Agencies. In 2022, the UN FLEET Portal also became fully operational, providing a state-of-the-art digital platform for Client Agencies to access UN FLEET’s full service offer, and digitally transforming how vehicle procurement services are managed by Client Agencies. The signing of these first 6 Global Service Agreements and the delivery of the first leased vehicles to Client Agencies is a concrete step forward in implementing the UN Reform efficiency agenda and a good example of United Nations entities finding ways to work more effectively together and manage a safer, more efficient, and sustainable fleet.
